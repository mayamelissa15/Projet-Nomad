Historique

Algiers the capital city of Algeria and has a long and fascinating history that dates back to ancient times. The city has been inhabited by various groups of people over the centuries, including the Phoenicians, Romans, Arabs, amazigh and French. Here is a brief overview of Algiers ... The city was founded by the Phoenicians in the 4th century BC, and was later conquered by the Romans in the 2nd century AD. During the Roman era, Algiers was an important center of trade and commerce, with a thriving port that served as a hub for the transportation of goods and people across the Mediterranean. In the 7th century, Algiers was conquered by the Arab armies of the Umayyad Caliphate, and became a major center of Islamic scholarship and learning. During this period, the city developed a rich culture and heritage, with many notable scholars, poets, and artists calling Algiers home. In the 16th century, Algiers was conquered by the Ottoman Empire, and became an important center of trade and commerce once again. The Ottomans built new harbors and fortifications in the city, which helped to further expand its economic and strategic importance. In the 19th century, Algiers was conquered by the French, who established a colonial administration and began a process of modernization and urbanization. The French built new roads, buildings, and infrastructure in the city, and also established a large naval base and administrative center in Algiers. During the 20th century, Algiers played a pivotal role in the struggle for Algerian independence from French colonial rule. The city was the site of many key political and military events during the Algerian War of Independence, which lasted from 1954 to 1962.

Casbah of Algiers

The Casbah is the old town of Algiers and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is a maze of narrow streets, traditional markets, and historic buildings that date back to the Ottoman era.

Notre Dame d'Afrique

This Catholic basilica is a landmark of Algiers and offers panoramic views of the city and the Mediterranean Sea.

Jardin d'Essai du Hamma

This botanical garden is one of the largest and most beautiful in the world, featuring a wide variety of exotic plants, trees, and flowers.

Museum of Antiquities

This museum features a collection of artifacts from the Roman, Punic, and Islamic eras, including mosaics, ceramics, and jewelry.

Algiers Cathedral

This historic cathedral is one of the most impressive examples of French colonial architecture in the city.

The National Museum of Fine Arts

This museum houses a large collection of Algerian art, including paintings, sculptures, and installations.

The Monument of the Martyrs

This war memorial commemorates the Algerian War of Independence and features a large bronze statue of a fallen soldier.

The Ketchaoua Mosque

This historic mosque is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is one of the oldest and most important religious sites in the city.
